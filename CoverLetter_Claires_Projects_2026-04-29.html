To the Claire's Transformation Leadership

Retail transformations fail less from missing technology and more from missing sequencing. Claire's has the brand equity, the customer base, and a defined window for radical change. The technology question is whether modernization is structured as a portfolio of measurable proof points or as a multi-year program with a board-deck end state. I prefer the former, and the projects below describe why.

Ransomware Incident Response (FVK Solutions / Cognesense, 2025). Sixty days from initial breach to full operational recovery. I led on-site containment, threat-actor negotiation, forensic data analysis, and re-architecture into a hardened, segmented infrastructure. The takeaway for Claire's: business continuity, PCI posture, and disaster-recovery muscle are built before they are tested, not after. The same crisis-execution discipline transfers directly to the 90-day Salesforce cutover and the holiday-season cutover protection that the 18-month plan requires.

HIMSS Information Architecture Modernization (DTIG, 2020). Worked with healthcare's information-management governing body to redesign a logical single source of truth on SharePoint and Dataverse - and measured a 28% improvement in knowledge-worker capacity. The relevance to Claire's: merchandising, store ops, and digital teams cannot make speed-to-market decisions without a shared data spine. I have built that spine in environments at least as complex as Claire's.

University of Chicago Medicine Multi-Farm Migration (DTIG, 2020-2021). Five SharePoint server farms migrated with zero operational downtime in a healthcare-critical environment. Modernization in retail is similarly unforgiving - store hours do not pause for cutover. The Claire's parallel is the multi-system live migration that the architecture requires: Salesforce CRM, Salesforce Commerce Cloud, fragmented store POS, and OMS replaced in sequence behind a HubSpot revenue spine, with read-only parallel-run discipline and twice-weekly reconciliation reports for thirty days after each cutover. I have run that pattern and know what it costs and what it saves.

AI Adoption Program (MMR, 2025). Embedded structured AI/ML adoption into a software-engineering organization, with KPIs linking AI usage to productivity and code-quality outcomes. The Claire's parallel is not chatbots; it is decision-making AI for inventory, demand forecasting, and assortment, starting with earrings in the Midwest at Day 90 and scaling to 60% of assortment by Month 12. That sequence converts the data spine into $20-30M of annual margin and $30-50M of working-capital release. That is where AI changes cash flow, not customer-facing copy.

Kaufman Hall Information Architecture Consolidation (2015-2019). Reduced annual IT spending by $1.2M while improving the platform serving 5,000+ users. Vendor strategy, financial discipline, and ROI tracking are not abstractions for me; they are how I have actually managed budgets.

The 18-month plan I would bring is exactly this kind of portfolio: a HubSpot-centered technology spine that retires Salesforce, the Generations and Princesses brand and loyalty program that reactivates 20-30 years of dormant equity in mothers and grandmothers, and 300+ store remodels with real omnichannel fulfillment, sequenced across $105-165M of investment against $500-800M of annualized revenue recovery at a 60-70% probability of disciplined execution. I would welcome the chance to walk through the dependency map and the proof points project by project.
